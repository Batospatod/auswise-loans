Frequently Asked Questions
Understanding SMSF Lending

An SMSF loan (or Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangement) allows your self-managed super fund to borrow money to purchase an investment property. The property is held in a separate custodian trust until the loan is fully repaid, at which point the SMSF acquires full title. Rental income and super contributions service the loan.

Depending on the lender and your fund's financials, you can borrow up to 90% of the property value for residential, and up to 80% for commercial properties. Maximum loan amounts range from $3M to $10M depending on the product and scenario. Your borrowing capacity is assessed based on the SMSF's income — including employer contributions, voluntary contributions, rental income, and investment returns.

Yes. You cannot live in a residential property purchased through your SMSF, and you cannot sell a residential property you personally own to your SMSF. Construction loans within an SMSF are also generally not available. Commercial property rules are more flexible — owner-occupied business premises and related party transactions are permitted in certain circumstances.

SMSF lending is more complex than standard home loans. Typically, it takes about one week to collate documents, and another week or so for assessment and approval. We recommend starting the process at least two weeks before actively searching for a property. Our team works to expedite this wherever possible.
